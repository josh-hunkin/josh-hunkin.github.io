Problem Solving



A time I got blocked on a simple problem

When I was a kid I used to play a lot of tennis. To master a forehand, backhand, serve etc, you have to have the right arm/body motion and the right grip. I found it very hard to master the backhand as a kid, a basic backhand. To solve this problem I stayed on the court for a while longer with my Dad just hitting the ball back and forth with him. He would video tape my shot and disect what I was doing right and wrong. We would review the video, I would fix my grip and then we would continue hitting. This reptitive practice is called continuous training (steady state training). I would perform this at a low to moderate intensity and it would drastically improve my game. I felt amazing after the practice because it is very fulfilling learning something new, especially after putting lots of work into whatever it is you're learning. It also does go to show that practice makes perfect.
