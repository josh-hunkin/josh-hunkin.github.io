MY PLAN



Where I am now:

I can create a basic website with html and css. I can create basic hover effects and shapes with css, as well as basic nav bars.



What I want to achieve by the end of foundations:

At the end of foundations I expect myself to know how to create basic animations, basic transition effects, and basic back end development.



How I will achieve this:

I will use resources I find very helpful such as freecodecamp.org, w3schools.com and YouTube. I will do this alongside my Dev Academy Foundations Handbook. I will track my time with Toggl, I will do this because it is satisfying to see something done within a reasonable time, not overdoing it or underdoing it. I will take breathers to sit back and assess how I will tackle the task if I am finding it challenging because the last thing you want to do is pointlessly destroy your energy reserves just to have nothing done. I will take a single 2 hour break a day, leaving the rest of the day to deep, uninterrupted focus on my tasks. I will plan my day accordingly the night before so when I get up I can grab my coffee and get stuck into the day.



My long/term goal and/or career pathway:

I will love to pursue a career in game/app development. I will also enjoy some ecommerce projects on the side. I will find companies that are known in the game development scene and secure roles, even if they are small, to gain experience in game development.



A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning:


STRENGTHS:

My strengths would be applying utmost detail to my work, but this can also be a limitation. Another strength I have is emotional intelligence.


LIMITATIONS:

My current limitation would be my focus for hours on end, but over time this muscle/habit is strengthening as I get back into the routine. I can see myself being fully capable of deep work by the end of foundations.



A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include - An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives:

I will manage my time with personal schedules/deadlines to ensure I finish my workload on time. This will allow smooth output from the whole team during bootcamp as no one will always be counting on me to finish. I will rely on myself and myself only to get my tasks done. I will train my ability to throw myself into deep work/focus. I have invested in noise cancelling headphones so I can remain working undistracted/uninteruptted.



What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you:

I will take a breather, write up a plan and assess what I am struggling with, and tackle problems one at a time. Taking a breather is important, I learned that when I started my blog and screwed up the css beyond repair. I just took a breather and that assisted me in taking the rational route as I could think clearly.



A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way:

I will seek help from my cohort and facilitator, as well as google. I have found w3schools to be very helpful. I will do this in a timely matter by seeking help before deadlines and making sure my cohort is available to assist and not overloaded with work themselves.



A description of what you expect from the facilitation team:

I don't expect much other than support and positivity to help keep the journey smooth and enjoyable. From what I know our facilitators have already exceeded my expectations.



BOOTCAMP PLAN



A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to bootcamp:


STRENGTHS:

I think my strengths will be my conistent hours put into learning and my prolonged periods of intense focus when I am fully engaged. I find myself not wanting to stop when something really grabs my attention and interest. I am also decent at html and css.


LIMITATIONS:

My current limitation would be Javascript. I am finding Javascript very difficult to understand as a whole, especially how to use it. Over time I am sure I will turn my limitation into a strength but for now I am worried I won't keep up with everyone.



What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think my biggest challenge will be working with peers. I have been socialising very little in the past few months and I have lost touch. It's not to say social skills can't be developed but it has been a bit since I have had proper interaction with mates etc. I remember it being much easier haha



What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to see my work with peers improve. I never really struggled with working in a pair/ with peers, but like I mentioned before it's been a while since I have met new people or interacted with new people. I think it's a very important skill to have and I am really looking forward to meeting like minded peers and learning with them.



What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

My only expectation is that we are provided with help if we are stuck. If I don't fully understand something and I can't find a solution, I expect help from the team to ensure I have a better understanding moving forward.



What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to put in the work whenever I can. This means I don't stop until I get it done. I expect myself to be resilient and to push through no matter how much I struggle. Mostly, I expect myself to just do my best no matter the circumstances.
